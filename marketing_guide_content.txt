SECTION 2: EXPERT PERSONA 
Expert Bio
Mark Pattison is a former NFL player, Emmy Award-winning documentary producer, mountaineer, business executive, author, podcaster, and speaker who has spent 15+ years helping high-achievers navigate the complexity of life after major transitions.
Born December 13th,1961, Mark is in the hall of fame at the University of Washington where he played wide receiver before being drafted into the NFL in 1985. After five seasons with the Los Angeles Raiders, New Orleans Saints, and Los Angeles Rams, Mark faced the identity crisis that defines many elite athletes: Who am I without the game?
What followed was two decades of entrepreneurial ventures, personal upheaval (including divorce), and a growing realization that his first summit, NFL glory, had been achieved, but he had no map for what came next.
At 50 years old, Mark made a decision that would redefine his life: he would become the first NFL player to climb the Seven Summits, the highest peak on each continent. This wasn't a bucket list item. It was a laboratory. A way to test whether the discipline, planning, and resilience that got him through football could be systematically applied to rebuilding a life.
Over the next several years, Mark summited Kilimanjaro, Elbrus, Aconcagua, Denali, Vinson Massif, Mt Kosciuszko, and ultimately Mt. Everest. Post the Seven, Mark has gone on to climb the Matterhorn, The Eiger, Mont Blanc, Cotopaxi, The Grand Teton, Mt Whitney and Mt Rainer. He documented the journey in an Emmy Award-winning film that chronicled not just the climbs, but the internal transformation they required.
While still actively climbing, Mark joined Sports Illustrated as a Senior Vice President and GM, where he applied the same methodical approach to turn around a struggling publication, taking it from #17 in its category to #1, generating over 1 billion page views. He has since launched multiple businesses, spoken at dozens of corporate and educational events, hosted a podcast featuring 280+ high-achieving guests, and written 2 books distilling his framework.
Mark lives in Sun Valley, Idaho, where he continues to ski, climb, and operate at the intersection of adventure and business. He is the father of a daughter with epilepsy, for whom he has raised over $150,000 through foundation work tied to his climbs.
He is not a motivational speaker. He is a guide for people who've already won once and need to figure out how to win again, differently.
