THE FRAMEWORK

HOW WE CLIMB DICTATES WHERE WE END UP.

Mark Pattison’s success—from the NFL to the Seven Summits—wasn’t built on talent alone. It was built on a repeatable system. When you strip away the pads and the climbing gear, the steps to conquering any "Everest" remain the same.

S

SEED

THE VISION

Everything begins with a single thought. A seed of possibility. Whether it's "I want to play in the NFL" or "I want to launch a business," you must first dare to plant the idea.

U

UNDERSTANDING

THE PREPARATION

A dream without a plan is just a wish. Understanding is about research, logistics, and honest assessment. What resources do you need? Who has done this before?

M

MINDSET

THE INNER GAME

The mountain doesn't care about your feelings. We work on resilience tools—visualization, positive self-talk, and "embracing the suck"—to ensure your mental state is your asset.

M

MOMENTUM

THE ACTION

You can't climb Everest by standing at Base Camp. We focus on consistency over intensity. Small, daily actions that compound over time to create an unstoppable force.

I

IDENTIFY

THE OBSTACLES

No plan survives contact with reality. We teach you to anticipate roadblocks and identify limiting beliefs that arise when the going gets tough.

T

TRAVERSE

THE PIVOT

Sometimes the direct path is blocked. You must be willing to move sideways to move up. It’s not quitting; it’s finding a new way to the same goal.

S

SUMMIT

THE EXECUTION

The final push. But the Summit isn't just about standing on top. It's about how you get down, what you learn, and how you use that view to find your next Everest.
