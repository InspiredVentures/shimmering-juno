The Framework

How we climb dictates where we end up.

Mark Pattison’s success—from the NFL to the Seven Summits—wasn’t built on talent alone. It was built on a repeatable system. When you strip away the pads and the climbing gear, the steps to conquering any "Everest" remain the same.

S

Seed

The Vision. Everything begins with a single thought. A seed of possibility. Whether it's "I want to play in the NFL" or "I want to launch a business," you must first dare to plant the idea.

In this phase, we identify what you actually want, stripping away the expectations of others to find your true Everest.

U

Understanding

The Preparation. A dream without a plan is just a wish. Understanding is about research, logistics, and honest assessment.

What skills do you lack? What resources do you need? Who has done this before? We map the route before we take the first step.

M

Mindset

The Inner Game. The mountain doesn't care about your feelings. You need a mind that is fortified against doubt, fear, and fatigue.

We work on resilience tools—visualization, positive self-talk, and "embracing the suck"—to ensure your mental state is your greatest asset, not your liability.

M

Momentum

The Action. You can't climb Everest by standing at Base Camp. Momentum is about taking that first step, and then the next.

We focus on consistency over intensity. Small, daily actions that compound over time to create an unstoppable force.

I

Identify

The Obstacles. No plan survives contact with reality. Storms will hit. Equipment will break. You will get tired.

We teach you to anticipate roadblocks and identify limiting beliefs that arise when the going gets tough, so you aren't blindsided by them.

T

Traverse

The Pivot. Sometimes the direct path is blocked. You must be willing to move sideways to move up.

Traversing is about adaptability. It’s not quitting; it’s finding a new way to the same goal. It requires flexibility and ego-checking.

S

Summit

The Execution. The final push. This is where preparation meets opportunity.

But the Summit isn't just about standing on top. It's about how you get down, what you learn, and how you use that view to find your next Everest.
