The Weekend

More than a conference. A call to action.

D1

DAY ONE: CONTEXT & FOUNDATION

FRIDAY NIGHT

You’ll hear from a small group of keynote speakers who’ve built real things and navigated real transitions. Honest stories about what worked, what didn’t, and what they learned the hard way.

Then we set the weekend up properly: how the SUMMITS methodology works, how your trail groups will run, and how to use these three days to get clear on what matters next.

16:00
Check-in & Gear Drop Get settled in your room and pick up your gear pack at the main lodge.
18:00
Welcome Dinner: "The False Summit" Casual dining where we break the ice and set the tone for the weekend.
20:00
Fireside Session: Mark Pattison Mark shares his personal journey and the origin of the SUMMITS methodology.

YOU’LL WALK AWAY WITH:

  • • A clear understanding of the SUMMITS roadmap and how we’ll apply it across the weekend
  • • Your first draft of an Everest worth pursuing now, in business, life, or both
  • • Your trail group and pace track (Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced)
  • • The rhythm for the weekend so you know exactly what’s coming and how to show up well

The speakers don’t disappear after they talk. You’ll spend real time with them on the trails the next day.

D2

DAY TWO: CONNECTION & MOMENTUM

SATURDAY

Saturday is where the Summit becomes different. The morning happens on the trails: guided hikes from Sun Valley Lodge with beginner, intermediate, and advanced options. Each group is led by a keynote speaker, so the conversations continue outside the room, where people are more present and less performative.

In the afternoon we bring it back inside and turn what surfaced into something practical: priorities, systems, and a plan you can execute when you get home.

08:30
HIKE 1: THE CONNECTION HIKE A non-technical but rigorous ascent to get blood moving and barriers down.
12:00
Lunch at The Lodge Refuel with a high-performance meal at the main lodge.
14:00
Workshop: Defining Your Everest Interactive session to identify your personal or professional 'Everest'.
19:00
Team Dinner Reflecting on the day's work with your trail group.

YOU’LL WALK AWAY WITH:

  • • Time with a keynote speaker in a small group setting, not a quick hello after a session
  • • Real connection with peers you’ll actually want to stay in touch with
  • • A clearer sense of what’s been stalling you and what needs to change
  • • A draft 90-day Summit plan: priorities, next steps, and simple metrics to stay honest

The goal isn’t to impress anyone. It’s to leave with momentum you can measure.

D3

DAY THREE: COMMITMENT & THE RETURN

SUNDAY

Sunday is about integration. We begin with an optional early reset, then we bring the group together to lock in your commitments and make the plan real.

This is where you choose what you’re taking back into everyday life: what you’ll protect, what you’ll stop, and what you’ll do next. We close by building your rope team for the months ahead, so you leave with support, not just inspiration.

08:00
HIKE 2: THE CLARITY HIKE Solidifying the plan. Silence and focus.
10:00
Final Workshop: The Traverse Plan Mapping out the specific steps to traverse from where you are to where you want to be.
12:00
Closing Lunch & Departure One last meal together before we depart.
14:00
The Descent: Application Workshop Practical application tools for when you return to 'sea level'.
16:00
Closing Ceremony & Departure Final remarks and commitment to the rope team.

YOU’LL WALK AWAY WITH:

  • • Your final 90-day plan, tightened and committed
  • • Your rope team: the people you’ll stay accountable to after Sun Valley
  • • A clear set of non-negotiables around focus, energy, and follow-through
  • • A sense of direction that holds when the noise comes back

The weekend ends, but the point is what happens next.

ESSENTIAL GEAR

01. BROKEN-IN BOOTS: New boots = blisters. Bring what you trust.
02. DAY PACK (20-30L): For water, layers, and snacks.
03. HARD SHELL LAYERS: Weather turns fast at 9,000ft. Bring a waterproof rain shell and a puffer/mid-layer. No cotton.
04. HYDRATION: 2 Liters minimum capacity. Water bladder (CamelBak) or Nalgene bottles.
05. SUN PROTECTION: High altitude sun is unforgiving. Sunglasses, hat, and sunscreen are mandatory.
06. TREKKING POLES (OPTIONAL): Helpful for the descent if you have bad knees, but not required.
07. TRAIL FUEL: Energy gels, bars, or nuts. High calorie, easy to eat while moving.
08. BLISTER KIT: Moleskin or Compeed. Address hotspots immediately before they become problems.

BASE CAMP & EVENING

01. MOUNTAIN CASUAL: Technical pants, flannels, comfortable shoes. We prioritize warmth and mobility over style.
02. ANALOG TOOLS: A dedicated notebook and pen. You will be writing a lot.
03. RECOVERY: Any personal supplements or recovery tools (foam roller, etc) you swear by.
04. SWIMWEAR: The lodge has heated outdoor pools. You will want to use them.
05. EVENING WARMTH: A beanie or warm hat for the evenings around the fire.

FIELD NOTES (FAQ)

How fit do I need to be?

You should be comfortable walking 3-5 miles on uneven terrain. We have pace groups for all levels, but this is an active weekend. If you can walk an 18-hole golf course, you can do this.

Is altitude a factor?

Yes. Sun Valley is at 5,900ft. Hydration starts on the plane ride here. Drink plenty of water before you arrive.

What is the dress code?

"Mountain Ready". Hiking pants, technical layers, and functional footwear. We want you comfortable and ready to move. We recommend leaving the denim at home.

Will I have cell service?

Yes, but we strongly encourage you to disconnect. Use this time to look up, not down.

What if it rains?

We hike. Unless there is lightning or severe danger, the schedule holds. Bring a waterproof shell.

Dietary Restrictions?

We cater to all standard requirements (GF, DF, V). You will be able to specify this during registration.
