THE WEEKEND

Sept 18-20, 2026 • Sun Valley, Idaho

MORE THAN A CONFERENCE. A CALL TO ACTION.

D1

DAY ONE: CONTEXT & FOUNDATION

FRIDAY: 17:00 - 22:00

17:00 // ARRIVAL & BASECAMP CHECK-IN Gear check, room assignment, and disconnecting from the grid. Phone lock-boxes available.
18:00 // THE "WHY" WELCOME Mark Pattison opens the weekend. Why are we here? What is the mountain asking of us?
19:30 // FIRE CIRCLE DINNER No networking cards. Just real food and the first question: "What is your Everest?"
21:00 // PRE-CLIMB BRIEFING Logistics for Saturday. Weather check. Team assignments (Beginner/Inter/Advanced).

You’ll hear from a small group of keynote speakers who’ve built real things and navigated real transitions. Honest stories about what worked, what didn’t, and what they learned the hard way.

YOU’LL WALK AWAY WITH:

  • • A clear understanding of the SUMMITS roadmap and how we’ll apply it across the weekend
  • • Your first draft of an Everest worth pursuing now, in business, life, or both
  • • Your trail group and pace track (Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced)
D2

DAY TWO: CONNECTION & MOMENTUM

SATURDAY: 07:00 - 21:00

07:00 // FUELING & MOBILITY High-protein breakfast. Optional guided stretch/mobility session.
08:00 // THE CLIMB Depart for trails. Groups split by pace. Keynote speakers rotate between groups. This is where the real work happens.
12:00 // SUMMIT LUNCH Packed lunch at the peak (or high point). Perspective shift. Solo reflection time.
14:30 // DESCENT & RECOVERY Return to base. Cold plunges available. Free time to nap or journal.
16:30 // WORKSHOP: THE EXECUTION PLAN Translating the hike insights into business/life strategy. "Traverse" obstacles workshop.
19:00 // THE FEAST Communal dinner. Stories from the trail. "Identify" session—calling out what holds us back.

Saturday is where the Summit becomes different. The morning happens on the trails. In the afternoon we bring it back inside and turn what surfaced into something practical: priorities, systems, and a plan you can execute when you get home.

YOU’LL WALK AWAY WITH:

  • • Real connection with peers you’ll actually want to stay in touch with
  • • A clearer sense of what’s been stalling you and what needs to change
  • • A draft 90-day Summit plan: priorities, next steps, and simple metrics to stay honest
D3

DAY THREE: COMMITMENT & THE RETURN

SUNDAY: 08:00 - 16:00

08:00 // RECOVERY YOGA Optional light movement to shake out the trail legs.
09:00 // FINAL WORKSHOP: ROPE TEAMS Locking in accountability. Who is watching your back when you leave? Establishing your monthly check-in intervals.
11:00 // CLOSING CEREMONY "Success" step. Celebrating the weekend, acknowledging the breakthroughs. Final remarks from Mark.
13:00 // FAREWELL LUNCH Communal lunch for one last conversation.
16:00 // SUMMIT CLOSE Official end of programming. Departures.

Sunday is about ensuring you don't leave the magic on the mountain. We build the "Rope Team" structure so you return home with a support system, not just a notebook full of ideas.

YOU’LL WALK AWAY WITH:

  • • Your final 90-day plan, tightened and committed
  • • Your rope team: the people you’ll stay accountable to after Sun Valley
  • • A clear set of non-negotiables around focus, energy, and follow-through

ESSENTIAL GEAR

  • 01. BROKEN-IN BOOTS Do not bring new boots. Your feet are your engine. Protect them.
  • 02. HARD SHELL LAYERS Weather turns fast at 9,000ft. Waterproof shell + puffer. No cotton.
  • 03. HYDRATION (2L+) CamelBak or Nalgenes. High altitude dehydrates you instantly.
  • 04. OFFLINE ESSENTIALS Notebook and pen. Digital noise is the enemy of clarity.
  • 05. SUN PROTECTION High altitude sun burns faster. Hat, sunglasses, sunscreen.
  • 06. DAY PACK 20-30L backpack to carry your layers, water, and notebook.

FIELD NOTES (FAQ)

HOW FIT DO I NEED TO BE?

Comfortable walking 3-5 miles at moderate inclines. If you can walk 18 holes of golf carrying your bag, you can do this. We have multiple pace groups.

WILL I HAVE CELL SERVICE?

Basecamp has wifi, but the trails are often out of range. We strongly encourage "Airplane Mode" for the entire weekend to maximize focus.

WHAT IS THE DRESS CODE?

"Mountain Ready." Function over fashion. Leave the blazer at home. Moisture-wicking fabrics are your friend.

HOW DO I HANDLE ALTITUDE?

Sun Valley is at ~6,000ft. Drink twice as much water as you think you need. Limit alcohol intake on the first night. We have electrolytes available.

DIETARY RESTRICTIONS?

Our culinary team caters to GF, Vegan, and Paleo. You will be asked for specific needs upon booking.
